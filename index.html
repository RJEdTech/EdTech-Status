About this dashboard. Status pages reflect what vendors choose to report and can lag actual outages by 30 minutes or more. Treat this as a first signal, not a definitive answer. If teachers report a problem and the tile is still green, the problem is real.

Why tools are tiered. Critical services (Microsoft sign-in, OneDrive/SharePoint, Outlook, Canvas) stop the school day if they're down. Core services (Teams, Blackbaud, Vivi, Flint) cause significant disruption but teaching can continue. Secondary tools affect individual classes at most. The summary banner weights these accordingly — a Canvas outage rings louder than a Gimkit outage even at the same severity.

Microsoft 365 is split into four tiles. Each tile reads from the same M365 RSS feed but filters for items relevant to that service (Entra/sign-in, OneDrive/SharePoint, Exchange/Outlook, Teams). Other M365 services Microsoft reports on (Power BI, Yammer, etc.) are intentionally not surfaced — they don't affect teaching.

Supporting infrastructure is context, not alert. AWS and Cloudflare underpin many of the tools above (Canvas, NoRedInk, Quizizz, and others). Issues at this layer don't necessarily mean anything is broken at Regis Jesuit — Cloudflare and AWS can have regional problems that never reach Aurora. These tiles exist to explain outages higher up. When a tool goes red and its infrastructure dependency is also red, the banner notes "possible upstream cause" so you know the root issue may not be the tool itself.
